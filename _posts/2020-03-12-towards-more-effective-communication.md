# <span style="color: #5e6970;">Towards More Effective Communication</span> {.h1-case-study-title}

\[/vc\_column\_text\]\[vc\_empty\_space height=&#8221;30px&#8221;\][vc\_column\_text]In the last few weeks, I’ve received several emails from co-workers that were well over a page long. As soon as I see that wall of text, I’m instantly filled with dread. After reading each message, I was left with a sense of confusion. What was I supposed to take from that? Is there something I am expected to do now?

&nbsp;

Communication is such an incredibly important part of our job. That’s doubly true in a remote company. My plan for today was to write a post about how to improve the messages you send. This morning, however, I saw [this article in The New York Times](https://www.nytimes.com/2020/03/04/smarter-living/your-colleagues-dont-read-anything-you-write-here-are-8-ways-to-change-that.html?algo=identity&fellback=false&imp_id=440723238&imp_id=709478411&action=click&module=Smarter%20Living&pgtype=Homepage). Instead of trying to make the same points again, I’m just going to link to it.
